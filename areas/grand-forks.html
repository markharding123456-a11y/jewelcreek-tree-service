Grand Forks Tree Service You Can Count On

Grand Forks sits at the confluence of the Kettle and Granby rivers, surrounded by forested hillsides that climb steeply from the valley floor. As the largest community in the Boundary region, the city has a mix of established residential neighborhoods, newer developments, commercial properties, and rural acreages on the outskirts. Each comes with its own tree management needs.

Jewel Creek Tree Service works throughout Grand Forks and the surrounding area, from the compact lots in the city core to the larger properties along the North Fork and Granby roads. We handle everything from a single backyard tree threatening a fence to multi-day clearing projects on development sites.

Urban Tree Challenges in Grand Forks

Mature trees in Grand Forks neighborhoods bring shade, beauty, and property value, but they also bring responsibility. Large cottonwoods along the Granby River corridor shed massive limbs, especially after spring windstorms. Aging birch and maple in older neighborhoods develop structural weaknesses that go unnoticed until a heavy snow load or summer storm brings a branch down on a roof or vehicle.

Working in town requires a different approach than working in the bush. Tight lot lines, overhead power lines, parked cars, fences, and neighboring structures all constrain how a tree can be safely removed or trimmed. Our crew is experienced in urban tree work, using rigging and sectional removal techniques that keep tight control over every piece of wood that comes down.

Property owners in Grand Forks should also be aware of municipal bylaws regarding tree removal on certain lots. We can advise you on what permits or approvals may be needed before work begins.
