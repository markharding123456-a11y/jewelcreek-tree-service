Wildfire Prevention & FireSmart

Protect your property before fire season arrives

Wildfire Is Not a Question of If, But When

The Boundary region of British Columbia lives with wildfire as a seasonal reality. Summers bring extreme heat, low humidity, and lightning storms that can ignite the surrounding forests with little warning. The devastating fire seasons of recent years have proven that properties in Christina Lake, Grand Forks, Rock Creek, Greenwood, and the surrounding valleys are not immune. The single most effective thing a property owner can do is prepare before fire season starts.

Jewel Creek Tree Service provides comprehensive wildfire prevention and FireSmart services designed to create defensible space around your home and reduce the fuel load on your property. We work with the FireSmart Canada guidelines to give your property the best possible chance of surviving a wildfire event.

Understanding Defensible Space

FireSmart principles divide the area around your home into priority zones. Zone 1, the critical first 10 metres from your structure, should be kept free of combustible vegetation, wood piles, and flammable materials. Zone 2, from 10 to 30 metres, requires thinned and spaced trees with pruned lower limbs and cleared brush. Zone 3, from 30 to 100 metres, benefits from forest thinning that breaks up the continuity of the tree canopy and removes dead standing timber.

Many properties in the Boundary have never been treated. Decades of fire suppression have allowed understory brush, dead fall, and ladder fuels to accumulate to dangerous levels. A ground fire that reaches this accumulated fuel can quickly climb into the canopy and become an unstoppable crown fire. Removing these ladder fuels is one of the most impactful steps you can take.

Local Knowledge Matters

Our crew understands the specific fire behavior patterns of the Boundary region. We know which slopes face the prevailing winds, where fire has historically moved through the valleys, and which tree species present the greatest risk. Ponderosa pine with its thick bark is naturally fire-resistant when properly spaced, while dense stands of Douglas fir with low-hanging branches act as fire ladders. We tailor every treatment plan to the specific conditions of your property.
