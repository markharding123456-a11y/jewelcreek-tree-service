Land Clearing

From dense bush to build-ready lots and fire-safe acreage

Clear the Way for What Comes Next

Whether you are preparing a building site, reclaiming overgrown pasture, creating defensible space around your home, or thinning a woodlot for forest health, Jewel Creek Tree Service has the crew and equipment to handle it. We clear land across the Boundary region from small residential lots to multi-acre rural properties.

Our approach is methodical. We walk the site with you first to understand exactly what stays and what goes. We mark boundaries, identify trees worth preserving, flag any environmental considerations, and then develop a clearing plan that gets the job done efficiently without collateral damage to neighboring trees or structures.

What We Clear

  • Building lot preparation for new construction
  • Forest thinning for improved stand health
  • Brush and understory removal
  • Fence line and boundary clearing
  • Driveway and access road clearing
  • Pasture and ranch land reclamation
  • FireSmart defensible space creation
  • Stump removal and site grading preparation
  • Debris chipping, hauling, or burning (where permitted)

Equipment and Methods

We match our equipment to the terrain and the scope of the project. Smaller residential lots may only require chainsaw crews and a chipper. Larger acreage jobs bring in skid steers, brush mowers, and hauling equipment. On steep or sensitive ground where heavy machinery would cause soil damage, we rely on hand-falling and cable systems to move material downhill without tearing up the landscape.

Fire Prevention Through Clearing

A significant portion of our land clearing work in the Boundary is driven by wildfire risk. Overgrown properties with dense brush, ladder fuels, and dead standing timber are a direct threat during fire season. Strategic thinning and brush removal creates the defensible space that can make the difference between a home that survives a wildfire and one that does not. Learn more about our dedicated wildfire prevention services.
